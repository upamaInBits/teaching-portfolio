XR EULA Prototype ↗

• Unity • C# • XR Interaction Toolkit • Meta Quest

Onboarding and age setup flow where users input age before entering the experience.

This project is an interactive VR prototype built in Unity for Meta Quest to explore how End User License Agreements can be redesigned for children. Instead of showing long legal text, the experience presents key concepts like safety, privacy, consent, and agreement understanding through guided interaction, simple language, visuals, audio, and child-friendly navigation.

The prototype uses a game-like structure with topic-based spaces, interactive learning moments, and a mini-game to help children better understand what they are agreeing to before entering an XR experience.

Focus: Child-friendly VR design, EULA comprehension, privacy awareness, consent in XR

XR Reading Usability Study ↗

• XR • Usability • HCI Research • User Studies

This project is a comparative usability study to evaluate how children interact with and comprehend reading content across Virtual Reality, Augmented Reality, and traditional web platforms.

The study involved children aged 6-11 years old and focused on measuring usability, cognitive load, and reading comprehension when interacting with long-form content such as stories and privacy policies.

Data was collected using NASA-TLX, surveys, observational notes, and post-task interviews to analyze user experience across different environments.

Skills demonstrated: Usability testing, research design, data collection, user analysis, HCI evaluation
